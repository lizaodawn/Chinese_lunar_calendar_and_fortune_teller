New Moon

新月 xīn yuè

If the new moon is visible, it is usually for a brief period of time around sunrise or sunset.

小楼新月，回首自纤纤。

In the small building under the crescent moon, one looks back to see the delicate and graceful view.

Waxing Crescent

蛾眉月 é méi yuè

The crescent moon will appear in the western sky shortly after sunset.

凉月如眉挂柳湾

The cool moon, like an eyebrow, hangs over the willow bay

First Quarter

上弦月 shàng xián yuè

The first quarter moon rises in the east in the early evening and lasts through the night until it sets in the west around midnight.

月落乌啼霜满天

The moon sets in midnight and the crows cry

Waxing Gibbous

盈凸月 yíng tú yuè

The first quarter moon rises in the east in the early evening and lasts through the night until it sets in the west around midnight.

Full Moon

满月 màng yuè

The full moon rises in the east in the evening and is visible all night until it sets in the west in the early morning.

明月几时有？把酒问青天

When will we have the bright moon? I raise my wine to ask the azure sky.

Waning Gibbous

虧凸月 kuī tú yuè

The full moon rises in the east in the evening, is visible throughout the night, and sets in the west in the early morning.

Last Quarter

下弦月 xià xián yuè

The last quarter moon rises in the east around midnight, is visible throughout the early morning

Waning Crescent

殘月 cán yuè

The waning moon rises in the east in the early morning and is visible until the sun rises.

杨柳岸，晓风残月。

By the willow bank, in the morning breeze and waning moon.
